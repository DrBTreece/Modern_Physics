There are many types of observations that can be made in physics. For the purposes of this discussion, we will focus on the observations of an event. Specifically, where and when something occurs.

$$ \text{Event} = (t, x, y, z) $$

Notice that in formulating the concept of an event, we have made a choice to put space and time on equal footing. This will become an important philosophical point as we continue our discussion of relativity.

How will we measure the quantities for recording an event? In practice, one would use a clock to measure the time of the event and a ruler to measure the position of the event.
